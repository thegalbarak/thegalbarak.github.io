Welcome!

My name is Gal Barak, and I'm a 25 year-old senior majoring in computer science and business.

Before coming to Brandeis University, I served in the Israel Defense Forces. As someone that has always been interested in leadership, I sought special roles for women, and made my way into the combat search and rescue unit. There I learned how to manage crisis situations, work under pressure, and lead effectively. Later, when I became a commander, I trained other soldiers in how to manage disasters, both in war and in peacetime.

Not long after completing my service, I won the Malkin Scholarship, an exclusive merit based scholarship at Brandeis University. I was particularly drawn to computer science because I love being able to execute the projects that I plan, and making a difference. As a woman in tech, my journey hasn't been easy, and I've chosen to give back to the community for the last two years by promoting computer science education for teenage girls through my work at Girls Who Code. I had the opportunity to expose about 50 high school girls to the world of computer science and assist them in their first steps in the industry. It has been an enlightening experience.

All of my experiences have helped me develop the skills that are important for every kind of company - I work well with others, know how to take responsibility and lead, and push myself to the limit. I am eager to explore and learn more every day, dedicated to make an impact, and always looking to take part in innovative projects that are changing the world.
